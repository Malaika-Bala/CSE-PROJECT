## Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

## 

## Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source. A typical phishing attack happens through fake emails or messages that appear to be from banks, online platforms, or government services.

## 

## For example, a student may receive an email claiming that their bank account will be blocked unless they verify their details immediately. The email contains a link to a fake website that looks identical to the real bank website. When the student enters their login details, the attacker captures the information and gains access to the account.

## 

## Phishing mainly targets students, working professionals, and elderly users who may not recognize fake links or emails. The consequences can include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

## 

## This type of cybercrime is dangerous because it relies on human error rather than technical hacking. Therefore, awareness and caution are essential to prevent such attacks.

# Prevention-checklist

## Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

## 

## \- Do not click on suspicious links or emails

## \- Always verify the sender before sharing information

## \- Use strong and unique passwords

## \- Enable two-factor authentication

## \- Never share OTPs or banking details

## \- Check website URLs carefully before logging in

## \- Avoid using public Wi-Fi for financial transactions

## \- Regularly update your apps and devices
